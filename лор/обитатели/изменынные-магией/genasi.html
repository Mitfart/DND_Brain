Genasi

Conjuration is the school of creation, and so genasi born of magic from this school often emit arcane echoes of their own limbs, with glowing, dreamlike echoes of arms floating behind them. By concentrating their inherent arcane magic, conjured genasi can make these arcane limbs material, being able to use them in combat, along with their natural set of limbs, making them particularly well-suited as martial combatants, although they have been very famous conjured genasi spellcasters that use their hand echoes to speed up the casting of somatic components far past what any other caster could muster.

Necromancy is the school that holds power over the dead, and so genasi born of magic from this school are born with entropy and death as a core part of their being. It's common for necromantic genasis to manifest arcane echoes in the form of will of the wisps but it's just as common for whatever limbs were used in the somatic components of the spell that made them into genasi to be completely skeletal, or for the flesh of that limb to be translucent, revealing the skeleton underneath. Particularly unlucky necromantic genasi emit a faint smell of rot themselves. Just like the often maligned school of magic necromantic genasis are tied to, these genasis are often unfairly maligned too.
and many hide their nature so as to avoid the fearful stares and hushed whispers of the general public, and worse, active persecution or accusations of an undead nature.

In contrast, abjuration genasi are often welcomed because of the spell that gave them their nature. Abjuration is the school of magical protection, and so, abjurative genasi manifest echoes of translucent shields around their body, most often around the parts involved in the somatic components of the spell that made them into genasi. Abjurative genasi can be recognized by the echoey shields circling their hands and arms, or even around their head in the case of somatic components. The reputation of the school of abjuration grants these genasis a leg up compared to others tied to more contentious schools of magic. But nothing about being born touched by abjuration magic makes these genasi more likely to protect or uphold good, and examples of those that have used their perceived benevolence for evil plague history.

Transmutation is the school that holds power over magic that seeks to change, and so transmutative genasi see their forms mutate and shift subtly but constantly. This effect is often contained to whatever limbs were used during the casting process of the spell that influenced them. Hands changed color, shape, an apparent age, an apparent species. Their voice changes too, most often going from masculine to feminine to acquiring an indeterminate quality to it, to even sounding more like a choir if verbal components were used in the casting of the spell that made them into what they are. Some transmutative genasi see their entire forms change, instead of specific body parts, in just a much more gradual transformation, and some are said to even be able to harness the transmutative energy within to undergo this change at will.

Divination is the school that holds power over seeing, across far distances and even towards the future and the past. Genasi affected by this school are theorized to be the inspiration of the common concept across popular culture of the Third Eye.

Depending on the form of divination used in the spell, it's not uncommon for Divinatory Genasi to have echoes of constellations floating around them, if the diviner that cast the spell that made them into Genasi used the stars for their divination, although cases of echoes in the form of tarot cards and even dice have been recorded.

Enchantment is the school that holds power over magic that seeks to exert influence. And so Genasi shaped by the School of Enchantment are known for two things. The first is their capability to not only blend in with the general population, their manifestation being much more discreet than those of their siblings, but also the ability they have to charm those around them, even without trying. Beauty is in the eye of the beholder, and the enchanted Genasi appearance is different depending on who is looking at them at any given time. The changes from observer to observer are not dramatic, but details will change to make the Genasi look as appealing as possible to the one observing them. granting them an ethereal attractiveness to anyone that lays eyes on the genasi. The second trademark of this specific genasi is their scent, and it behaves very similarly to their looks. Two people will describe wildly different smells when talking about the same genasi, but both will agree that they found their smell particularly pleasant and attractive. Powerful enchanted genasi will even manifest arcane echoes in the shape of plumes of smoke, like the one emanating out of a burning stick of incense. The ease with which Enchanted Genasi blend in with the general populace makes their existence much easier than that of their siblings, but also makes them uniquely suited to use these abilities for less than savoury ways, which has garnered an ill reputation for these Genasi, deservedly or not.

Illusory Genasi, on the other hand, are the other side of the coin of Enchanted Genasi. Illusion is the school that holds power over magic that seeks to obfuscate and hide, and so Illusory Genasi have a very hard time being perceived.
Some illusory genasi have body parts like fingers or in some rare cases, necks, legs or even heads be permanently invisible, only a faint outline of them being perceivable to the naked eye. But most illusory genasi are just hard to describe. It isn't invisibility, people can see them. But when these people are asked to describe details of the genasi's appearance, the witness have an extremely hard time actually describing anything specific, not managing anything more than the height or general build. The contours of the illusory genasi are plain to see when you are looking at one. The sound of their voice is clear. But once you tear your eyes away from them, you are simply unable to recall what you were hearing and seeing with any degree of specificity. You knew they were there, you just don't know who they were. Some illusory genasi have chosen to combat this by wearing particularly outrageous and recognizable outfits, evoking what is called the peacock effect so that the genasi may be spared the pain of their loved ones not being able to recognize them. Arcane Genasi have acted like living spell scrolls, eminences of the Arcane, and some have even been used by particularly evil mages as Arcane components for the same type of grand magic that birthed those same Genasi. Some Arcane Genasi set out to discover the spell that was transcribed into their flesh. While they do so to better understand the magic that has formed them into what they are, or because they resent their nature and wish to put an end to the spellcaster that cast it, it is for the Arcane Genasi to decide.
